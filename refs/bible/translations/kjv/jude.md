## <a id="kjv-jude"></a>The General Epistle of Jude


### <a id="kjv-jude-1"></a>Chapter 1

Jude, the servant of Jesus Christ, and brother of James, to them
that are sanctified by God the Father, and preserved in Jesus Christ,
and called:<a id="kjv-jude-1-1"></a><sup>1</sup> Mercy unto you, and peace, and love, be multiplied.<a id="kjv-jude-1-2"></a><sup>2</sup>

Beloved, when I gave all diligence to write unto you of the common
salvation, it was needful for me to write unto you, and exhort you
that ye should earnestly contend for the faith which was once
delivered unto the saints.<a id="kjv-jude-1-3"></a><sup>3</sup>

For there are certain men crept in unawares, who were before of
old ordained to this condemnation, ungodly men, turning the grace of
our God into lasciviousness, and denying the only Lord God, and our
Lord Jesus Christ.<a id="kjv-jude-1-4"></a><sup>4</sup>

I will therefore put you in remembrance, though ye once knew this,
how that the Lord, having saved the people out of the land of Egypt,
afterward destroyed them that believed not.<a id="kjv-jude-1-5"></a><sup>5</sup>

And the angels which kept not their first estate, but left their
own habitation, he hath reserved in everlasting chains under darkness
unto the judgment of the great day.<a id="kjv-jude-1-6"></a><sup>6</sup>

Even as Sodom and Gomorrha, and the cities about them in like
manner, giving themselves over to fornication, and going after strange
flesh, are set forth for an example, suffering the vengeance of
eternal fire.<a id="kjv-jude-1-7"></a><sup>7</sup>

Likewise also these filthy dreamers defile the flesh, despise
dominion, and speak evil of dignities.<a id="kjv-jude-1-8"></a><sup>8</sup>

Yet Michael the archangel, when contending with the devil he
disputed about the body of Moses, durst not bring against him a
railing accusation, but said, The Lord rebuke thee.<a id="kjv-jude-1-9"></a><sup>9</sup>

But these speak evil of those things which they know not: but
what they know naturally, as brute beasts, in those things they
corrupt themselves.<a id="kjv-jude-1-10"></a><sup>10</sup>

Woe unto them! for they have gone in the way of Cain, and ran
greedily after the error of Balaam for reward, and perished in the
gainsaying of Core.<a id="kjv-jude-1-11"></a><sup>11</sup>

These are spots in your feasts of charity, when they feast with
you, feeding themselves without fear: clouds they are without water,
carried about of winds; trees whose fruit withereth, without fruit,
twice dead, plucked up by the roots;<a id="kjv-jude-1-12"></a><sup>12</sup> Raging waves of the sea,
foaming out their own shame; wandering stars, to whom is reserved the
blackness of darkness for ever.<a id="kjv-jude-1-13"></a><sup>13</sup>

And Enoch also, the seventh from Adam, prophesied of these,
saying, Behold, the Lord cometh with ten thousands of his saints,<a id="kjv-jude-1-14"></a><sup>14</sup> To execute judgment upon all, and to convince all that are ungodly
among them of all their ungodly deeds which they have ungodly
committed, and of all their hard speeches which ungodly sinners have
spoken against him.<a id="kjv-jude-1-15"></a><sup>15</sup>

These are murmurers, complainers, walking after their own lusts;
and their mouth speaketh great swelling words, having menâ€™s persons in
admiration because of advantage.<a id="kjv-jude-1-16"></a><sup>16</sup>

But, beloved, remember ye the words which were spoken before of
the apostles of our Lord Jesus Christ;<a id="kjv-jude-1-17"></a><sup>17</sup> How that they told you
there should be mockers in the last time, who should walk after their
own ungodly lusts.<a id="kjv-jude-1-18"></a><sup>18</sup>

These be they who separate themselves, sensual, having not the
Spirit.<a id="kjv-jude-1-19"></a><sup>19</sup>

But ye, beloved, building up yourselves on your most holy faith,
praying in the Holy Ghost,<a id="kjv-jude-1-20"></a><sup>20</sup> Keep yourselves in the love of God,
looking for the mercy of our Lord Jesus Christ unto eternal life.<a id="kjv-jude-1-21"></a><sup>21</sup>

And of some have compassion, making a difference:<a id="kjv-jude-1-22"></a><sup>22</sup> And others
save with fear, pulling them out of the fire; hating even the garment
spotted by the flesh.<a id="kjv-jude-1-23"></a><sup>23</sup>

Now unto him that is able to keep you from falling, and to
present you faultless before the presence of his glory with exceeding
joy,<a id="kjv-jude-1-24"></a><sup>24</sup> To the only wise God our Saviour, be glory and majesty,
dominion and power, both now and ever. Amen.<a id="kjv-jude-1-25"></a><sup>25</sup>
